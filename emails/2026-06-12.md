### How Fast are Prolly Trees?

For a long time, we've wondered how fast [Prolly Trees](https://www.dolthub.com/docs/architecture/storage-engine/prolly-tree/) are in practice compared to B-trees. Algorithmically, [the performance is comparable, log(n) seek,](https://www.dolthub.com/docs/architecture/storage-engine/prolly-tree/#motivation) but what are the constants? It was always hard to tell with Dolt itself because Dolt and MySQL use different SQL engines.

But [DoltLite](https://github.com/dolthub/doltlite), my new [vibe-coded master work](https://www.dolthub.com/blog/2026-03-24-a-week-in-gas-town/), is SQlite from the B-tree up. DoltLite is written in C the same language as SQLite, removing another confounder. So, [we finally have a fair race](https://www.dolthub.com/blog/2026-06-08-how-fast-is-doltlite). 

In DoltLite, Prolly Trees are a ~20% tax on reads and a ~67% tax on writes for in-memory databases, the fairest comparison. On committed writes, you pay a 400% tax. SQLite is very fast so we're talking about microseconds but you do pay a tax for version control.
