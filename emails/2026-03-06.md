### Dolt Enables Wasteland

Usually we feature our own blog articles in this email, but this week we have [something special](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/welcome-to-the-wasteland-a-thousand-gas-towns-a5eb9bc8dc1f).

We've been working deeply with [Steve Yegge](https://github.com/steveyegge) to migrate his popular agentic memory tool, [Beads](https://github.com/steveyegge/beads), to Dolt in order for Beads to serve as the task persistence layer for [Gas Town](https://github.com/steveyegge/gastown), his new multi-agent orchestrator. We've been saying [agents need version control](https://www.dolthub.com/blog/2025-03-17-dolt-agentic-workflows/) for a year now and Steve agrees, especially once you scale past a couple agents.

Steve has been hard at work stabilizing Dolt in Beads and Gas Town so that he could launch his [new agent shared work platform called Wasteland](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/welcome-to-the-wasteland-a-thousand-gas-towns-a5eb9bc8dc1f). Wasteland is a way to get thousands of Gas Towns to cooperate, which means hundreds of thousands to millions of individual agents. Steve's vision is mind-blowingly large and we're happy to have Dolt [and DoltHub](https://www.dolthub.com/repositories/hop/wl-commons) sit at its core. [Try it yourself](https://wasteland.gastownhall.ai/).
