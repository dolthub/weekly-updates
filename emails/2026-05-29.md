### Dumbo Gets Transactions

[Neil](https://www.dolthub.com/team#neil) and some robot pals continue to grind full time on [Dumbo](https://github.com/dolthub/dumbodb), our MongoDB-compatible version-controlled Document database. This week, we announced [transaction support](https://www.dolthub.com/blog/2026-05-26-dumbodb-transactions/) which is a little strange in stock MongoDB. Transactions only work in replica sets. Dumbo just supports them out of the box.

I know this is a SQL crowd but Dumbo needs a handful of early adopters to help guide the product. Where the Node.js folks at? If you're interested in Dumbo for a use case, reply to this email. Help a CEO in need out.

### Dolt Has Conflicts

A user asked how other users [solve conflicts programmatically](https://www.dolthub.com/blog/2026-05-27-conflicts-in-the-wild/). It's a topic we've never written about because the answer is kind of lame. Most users design their Dolt applications so merges don't conflict. But, if they do have merge conflicts, they often note them and resolve them out of band in a cleanup process. The dream of having business logic to handle conflict cases is only achieved by a special few. If that's you, give yourself a pat on the back from me.
