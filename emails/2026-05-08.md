### Dumbo

We've heard for years from users that Dolt's version control features would be incredibly useful in a document database. We always hemmed and hawed abut modern SQL engines supporting JSON. [Dolt's JSON support is best in class](https://www.dolthub.com/blog/2025-01-30-json-mongodb-vs-dolt/). Yadda yadda. 

But, one thing we've also learned building Dolt for almost eight years are people are pretty brand-loyal to their database engines. MySQL people love MySQL. Postgres is the best. SQLite everywhere and always. Have JSON. Need Mongo. For this reason, we built [Dolt](https://www.doltdb.com), [Doltgres](https://www.doltgres.com), and recently [DoltLite](https://www.doltlite.com), a version-controlled database for the three most popular open source flavors of SQL.

What about JSON documents? We didn't forget about you. We were waiting and watching the ecosystem evolve. [FerretDB](https://www.ferretdb.com/) plus agents gave us an opening and, like an elephant with ears for wings, we flew using [the power of Gas Town](https://www.dolthub.com/blog/2026-04-16-two-weeks-in-gastown/). 

[Introducing Dumbo](https://www.dolthub.com/blog/2026-05-07-announcing-dumbodb/), a version-controlled MongoDB-compatible document database. At least, throw it [a star on GitHub](https://github.com/dolthub/dumbodb). At most, try it and tell us if you like it.
