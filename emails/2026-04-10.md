### DoltLite Improves

You may know there is an ongoing holy war between [Vibe Code and Trad Code](https://www.dolthub.com/blog/2026-03-26-vibe-code-vs-trad-code/). The frontline here at DoltHub is [DoltLite](https://github.com/timsehn/doltlite), our entirely vibe-coded fork of SQLite with Dolt-like version control features. 

This week I forced [Aaron to review it](https://www.dolthub.com/blog/2026-04-08-doltlite-code-review-highlights) leading to his comment above. I had already been working hard [making DoltLite better](https://www.dolthub.com/blog/2026-04-09-improving-doltlite) and Aaron's review sparked [a couple of major releases](https://github.com/timsehn/doltlite/releases).

Do you want to be part of the Vibe Code revolution? Try [DoltLite](https://github.com/timsehn/doltlite)! Let's stick it to the mean Trad Coders.

### Doltgres Works with Prisma

Speaking of Trad Code, we still write artisanal, small-batch software here at DoltHub: Dolt, Doltgres, and all our other products. This week we're proud to announce that [Doltgres works with Prisma](https://www.dolthub.com/blog/2026-04-06-doltgresql-prisma), a popular Javascript framework. We're getting close to [Doltgres 1.0](https://www.dolthub.com/blog/2025-10-16-state-of-doltgres/#looking-forward-to-10). 

### Beads Classic

A lot of you are on this mailing list because you are fans of Steve Yegge's [Beads](https://github.com/gastownhall/beads), [Gas Town](https://github.com/gastownhall/gastown), and [Wasteland](https://github.com/gastownhall/wasteland). These tools heavily rely on Dolt for task persistence. As part of [Beads going 1.0](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/gas-town-from-clown-show-to-v1-0-c239d9a407ec), [Dustin restored embedded Dolt to Beads](https://www.dolthub.com/blog/2026-04-02-restoring-beads-classic) or as Steve likes to call it: Beads Classic. No Dolt server required for a simple Beads use case. Keep the feature requests coming.
